ABOUT THE INSTITUTION

MANIT Bhopal

Maulana Azad National Institute of Technology (MANIT), an Institute of National Importance, was formerly known as Maulana Azad College of Technology (MACT). Institute is named after the great scholar and the 1st Education Minister of Govt. of India, Maulana Abul Kalam Azad. The Govt. of India and Govt. of Madhya Pradesh jointly sponsored the Institute in 1960 to attract bright young engineering students across the country.
Institute is successfully producing the highest quality skilled manpower to cope up with the challenges of modern technological advances with more than 200 faculties and 5000 students. The Institute offers 09 UG and 30 PG courses, and also Ph.D. research programmes.

Announcement & Call for Papers

ICAMT 2024

The International Conference on Materials for Advanced Technology brings together the world's leading academic scholars, researchers, and industrialists to exchange and share information on all aspects of advancements in materials science and technology. In addition, the conference provides a premier interdisciplinary forum for researchers, practitioners, and educators to explore and discuss the most recent innovations, trends, and concerns as well as practical challenges encountered and solutions adopted in their respective fields. In todayâ€™s rapidly changing world of materials, there are many fascinating and ever-growing options. The understanding of such a wide variety of materials demand an interdisciplinary knowledge of disciplines such as materials science and engineering, mechanical engineering, physics, chemistry, biology and mathematics.

ICAMT-2024 will feature some of the world's leading experts as keynote speakers and poster presenters. These will inspire the researchers with an exposure to a variety of topics and provide opportunity for discussions with stalwarts of materials research.

Scope

The conference will have oral and poster sessions covering topics such as those given below:

Shape memory alloys
Functional materials
Electronic materials and devices
Advanced ceramics
Advanced composites
Self-healing materials
High temperature materials
New energy materials
Energy storage materials
Additive manufacturing
Biomaterials
Nanomaterials and structures
Advanced processing and joining techniques
Hydrogen production, storage and applications

Events Speakers

Dr. Rajesh Kumar

Professor - Indian Institute of Technology (IIT)

Dr. Maria Garcia

Professor - Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)

Dr. Maria Garcia is a distinguished professor in the field of materials science and engineering at MIT. She specializes in biomaterials and their applications in medical devices and tissue engineering.

Dr. John Smith

Research Scientist - European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN)

Dr. John Smith is a particle physicist and research scientist at CERN. He is involved in experiments related to the study of fundamental particles and their interactions using high-energy particle accelerators.

Dr. Li Wei

Professor - Tsinghua University

Dr. Li Wei is a prominent materials scientist at Tsinghua University, specializing in the development of advanced materials for energy storage applications. His research focuses on lithium-ion batteries and supercapacitors.

Dr. Mia Roberts

Research Scientist - National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA)

Dr. Mia Roberts is an aerospace engineer and research scientist at NASA. She specializes in materials for spacecraft and space exploration missions, focusing on lightweight and durable materials for extreme environments.

Dr. Akio Yamamoto

Professor - Kyoto University

Dr. Akio Yamamoto is a leading materials scientist at Kyoto University, specializing in the development of functional materials for electronic and photonic applications. His research focuses on semiconductor materials and devices.

Dr. Carlos Fernandez

Research Scientist - European Space Agency (ESA)

Dr. Carlos Fernandez is a materials scientist and researcher at the European Space Agency. He focuses on materials for space exploration and satellite technology, including thermal protection materials and advanced composites.
